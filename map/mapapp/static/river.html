The Krishna River is one of the major rivers of India, flowing through the states of Maharashtra, Karnataka, Telangana, and Andhra Pradesh. It originates from the Western Ghats near Mahabaleshwar and travels about 1,400 kilometers before emptying into the Bay of Bengal. The river is an important source of irrigation and drinking water, supporting agriculture and livelihoods along its banks. It also holds cultural and religious significance for the people living in the region.
